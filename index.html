    About


    Hi, I'm Carson! I'm a game designer and programmer with a passion for crafting immersive, cinematic experiences that blend innovative storytelling with engaging gameplay. Whether through intricate level design, deep systems programming, or interactive narrative elements, I strive to create games that captivate players and leave lasting impressions.

    With experience in Unity, Unreal Engine, and a range of programming languages, my work spans VR simulations, indie game projects, and interactive learning experiences. I thrive on technical problem-solving, creative iteration, and designing worlds that feel alive.

    Currently, I’m completing my degree in Games, Interactive Media, and Mobile Technology at Boise State University while working on projects that push the boundaries of interactive experiences. My recent work includes developing educational games, VR safety simulations, and an indie studio project.

    Beyond game development, I find inspiration in film, outdoor exploration, and dynamic storytelling, all of which shape my creative approach. I’m always looking to collaborate, innovate, and push the medium forward.

    Client Projects

    IdaGem

    Collaborators: GIMM Works, Wassmuth Center for Human Rights, Department of Homeland Security

    Roles: Project Lead, Programmer

    About: IdaGem is a gamified 4th-grade history curriculum designed in collaboration with Idaho teachers. It transforms traditional lessons into an interactive experience, with extensive lessions, students exploring a virtual Idaho Anne Frank Memorial, and completing educational minigames. Aligned with state standards and currently used in classrooms, IdaGem is also being submitted for an educational game award.

    Website

    Geoscience Exhibit

    Collaborators: Dr. Megan Cattau, Mae Bridgeman, Kaj Forney, Chase Franklin, Mitchell Oliarny, Jayden Quallio

    Roles: Programmer, UI, Data Conversion

    About: This application has been developed for an exhibit at the 2024 Personal Structures Exhibition in Venice, Italy. It displays geological drone data gathered by Boise State University's Human-Environment Systems department in an interactive manner. Exhibitors are able to immerse themselves in the environment while learning about invasive plant species in Idaho.

    Vocab Verse

    Collaborators: Jacob Thrall, Konner Pierce, Rachael Nordby, Kaj Forney, Chase Franklin

    Roles: Lead Programmer

    About: Vocab Verse is an iOS app designed to help students with dyslexia and autism improve word recognition through interactive, image-based learning. Built in Swift, it reinforces vocabulary comprehension with engaging visuals and structured exercises. Developed with a focus on accessibility, the app is currently being submitted for an Apple award.

    Games


    Six Shooter

    Roles: Designer, Programmer, Artist

    About: An old-school, VR roguelike, heavily inspired by Spaghetti Westerns of the mid-to-late 1960s. With your trusty revolver in hand, you must face off against waves of outlaws, to determine if you are truly the fastest gun in the west.

    With a focus on fast-paced, arcade-style gameplay, and a variety of levels and upgrades, each playthrough is unique and challenging to the last.

    A Thief's Dilemma

    Collaborators: Sam Lummus, Kevin Sherwood, Hank Davidson

    Roles: Project Lead, Lead Programmer, Artist, Animator, 3D Modeler, Level Designer, Sound Designer

    About: A story-based interactive comic book about a master thief who has access to powers related to quantum mechanics.
